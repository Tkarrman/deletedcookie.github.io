The Game

Co-op chaos with consequences

Players travel between infected villages, attempting to help its villagers by gathering resources, craft medicine, manage panic, and trying not to make everything worse.

Chaotic Cooperation

Dynamic co-op gameplay creates funny, stressful, emergent situations.

Dark Humor

A mix of creepy medieval horror and absurd comedy.

Consequences

Mistakes have visible effects on villagers, outbreaks, monsters, and the world.

World

Infected villages as chaotic playgrounds

Each village is a unique playground where plague doctors cure or aid as many residents as possible before dawn. Players rummage through bushes, tread mud, chase rats, and search every corner for items that might help or accidentally cause more trouble.

Characters

Tiny doctors. Big disasters.

The plague doctor

The player character is a tiny plague doctor summoned by a helpful witch. Their existence is to frantically run around villages, trying to help everyone, moving with quirky and chaotic animations. But… those tiny legs can only do so much. Sometimes the things they need are just out of reach and that’s when creativity becomes essential.

Sick villagers

The sick villagers will display various symptoms through both their appearance and behavior. It is up to the players to figure out what they need and how to get it. But prioritise wrong, and some sicknesses may spread: leaving you with more sick villagers than when you arrived. The witch will not be happy with you then.

Game Loop

A repeating cycle of treatment, panic, and travel

Accept a mission from the wandering Hut and travel toward frightened villagers, strange symptoms, and terrible decisions.
Investigate symptoms and diagnose illnesses, read villager behavior, surroundings, and disease clues.
Gather herbs and resources search in forests, swamps, graveyards, houses, and dangerous areas.
Craft medicine and treatments, combine ingredients in a shared cauldron. Correct recipes save lives; mistakes create new problems.
Every treatment has risk, reward, and comedy potential. A leg looks infected? CHOP it off! Swoosh! Problem solved… probably.
Manage panic, deaths, and reputation if the team loses control, the village becomes the threat.
Earn rewards, sell questionable remains, upgrade tools and prepare for the next village.
Leave the village behind and pretend the screaming must mean they’re happy the good work!
Features

Systems built for emergent stories

Investigation

Examine symptoms, surroundings, and disease patterns before disaster spreads.


Resource Gathering / Crafting

Gather resources to up in a shared cauldron. Good recipes save lives; bad recipes create chaos.


Surgery & Treatments

Use medicine, surgery, quarantine, and extreme methods to solve urgent situations.


Dragging & Disposal

Move heavy bodies alone or together, burn them, sell them, or save up to buy a corps wagon, the sports car of dead villager disposal.

Dynamic Events

Rat King, infestations, zombies, failed experiments, and escalating village disasters.

Stealth

When danger comes your way and it´s too late run; hide in the closest barrel, haystack, tall grass, or behind a close distracted friend.

The Hub

A Baba Yaga-inspired wandering hut that acts as home, workshop, quest board, and unsettling safe haven.

Market Trends

Why now?

Social co-op games, creator-driven content, and stylized horror-comedy continue to grow rapidly across Steam and streaming platforms.

Social Chaos is Growing

Games like Lethal Company, R.E.P.O., Content Warning, and Phasmophobia show strong demand for multiplayer experiences driven by communication, panic, and emergent storytelling.

Medieval & Plague Themes

Medieval horror, plague doctors, alchemy, witches, and dark folklore continue to perform strongly across games, social media, memes, and creator content due to their instantly recognizable visual identity.

Streamer-Friendly Design

Plague Crew is designed around unpredictable player interaction, failures, and shared disasters, systems that naturally create clips, reactions, and viral co-op moments.

PEGI 12 Target

Spooky, not graphic

The game uses stylized characters, exaggerated comedy, distant camera perspectives, and non-realistic effects. Surgery, plague, monsters, and disasters are presented as cartoon-like horror-comedy rather than realistic gore or prolonged suffering.

Audience

Friend groups, streamers, and co-op chaos fans

Designed for players who enjoy communication, improvisation, and emergent multiplayer moments. Comparable audience references include Lethal Company, R.E.P.O., Content Warning, Phasmophobia, and Overcooked.

Challenges

Networked chaos and finding the fun

Network games are challenging, but the team has experience and the tech demo shows promise using Photon Fusion and Photon Voice. The key design challenge is enhancing the co-op chaos so players remember the time they were chased by the Rat King or accidentally turned a village into a zombie horde.
