Crew

The plague is the easy part.

A chaotic 3D co-op game where tiny plague doctors travel between infected villages, diagnose diseases, craft questionable medicine, amputate when necessary, and try to survive the consequences of their own bad decisions.

GenreCo-op survival chaos
PlayersFriends, streamers, party groups
ToneDark comedy horror

A village is sick. Your team has one night to help. Every cure can become a catastrophe.

The fantasy

Players are summoned by a strange witch to rescue infected villages before dawn. They investigate symptoms, scavenge ingredients, use a shared cauldron, perform risky treatments, hide from consequences, and drag away the evidence when everything goes wrong.

01 · THE GAME

Chaotic cooperation with visible consequences.

The design is built around funny failure, stressful teamwork, and stories players want to retell.

Chaotic Cooperation

Players split up, shout discoveries, carry heavy objects together, miscommunicate recipes, and accidentally make everything worse.

Dark Humor

Creepy medieval plague fantasy meets absurd, physical comedy: tiny doctors, questionable medicine, corpse wagons, and witch-approved “solutions.”

Consequences

Wrong priorities spread sickness. Bad crafting poisons villagers. Unmanaged corpses can trigger zombies, monsters, panic, and reputation loss.

02 · WORLD

Each village is a chaotic playground.

Players rummage through bushes, mud, graveyards, forests, and homes looking for the one ingredient that might save everyone — or ruin everything.

The Plague Doctors

Tiny, frantic, expressive characters with quirky animations. Their small size turns the world into a problem-solving playground: climb, drag, hide, stack, carry, and improvise.

The Villagers

Sick villagers show symptoms through appearance and behavior. Players must observe, diagnose, prioritize, and treat them before sickness spreads or panic takes over.

03 · CORE LOOP

Simple to understand. Hard to survive.

The loop creates a clean pitch: arrive, investigate, gather, craft, treat, contain chaos, upgrade, repeat.

Arrive at an infected villageChoose a mission from the Hub and enter a new chaotic sandbox.
Investigate symptomsRead villager behavior, surroundings, and disease clues.
Gather resourcesSearch forests, swamps, graveyards, houses, and dangerous areas.
Craft medicineCombine ingredients in a shared cauldron. Correct recipes save lives; mistakes create new problems.
Treat, quarantine, operate, or improviseEvery treatment path has risk, reward, and comedy potential.
Manage panic, corpses, monsters, and reputationIf the team loses control, the village becomes the threat.
Return to the HubUpgrade tools, sell questionable remains, prepare for the next village.
04 · FEATURES

Systems that create memorable co-op stories.

The fun comes from the overlap: diagnosis, crafting, physics, panic, combat, stealth, and player error.

  • Investigation: examine symptoms and identify disease before it spreads.
  • Resource gathering: limited herbs and rare ingredients push players to split up and take risks.
  • Shared crafting: a team cauldron turns communication into comedy and danger.
  • Combat: clumsy last-resort fights against infected creatures, villagers, and supernatural threats.
  • Surgery: drastic medieval treatments can prevent spread — or become useful materials.
  • Dragging: bodies and heavy objects require teamwork, tools, or questionable corpse-wagons.
  • Events: Rat King, infestations, zombie outbreaks, failed experiments, and Frankenstein-like disasters.
  • Stealth: hide in barrels, hay, tall grass, or behind a distracted friend.
  • The Hub: a Baba Yaga-inspired walking hut for upgrades, quests, merchants, and unsettling discoveries.
05 · VISUAL DIRECTION

Readable, modular, creepy-cute medieval chaos.

Modular buildings support fast village variation while keeping a handcrafted, toy-like dark fantasy look.

08 · RISKS

The hard parts are known — and testable.

Networked co-op, emergent events, and “finding the fun” are the main challenges, so the roadmap focuses on early playtesting and vertical-slice proof.

Networking

Photon Fusion and Photon Voice are already part of the tech demo, reducing unknowns around sync and proximity communication.

Events

Events must be surprising without feeling random. The goal is controlled chaos that players can understand and blame each other for.

Fun discovery

Small playtests will identify which interactions create the strongest co-op stories, then production can double down on those systems.
